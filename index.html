Philosophy

AI should amplify,
not announce itself.

The best AI experiences are ones users can't quite describe — because the intelligence felt so natural they didn't notice it working. I design for that invisible moment.

My background in Visual Art and Mathematics gives me an unusual lens: I see systems as compositions and compositions as systems. That dual perspective shapes every product decision.

Principles
01
Ambient over assistive
AI that works without demanding attention earns more trust than AI that constantly announces what it's doing.
02
Trust lives in the details
Error messages, loading states, edge cases — the unsexy work is where user trust is built or broken.
03
Accessibility is good design
100% of accessibility users preferred Libra over traditional conference rooms. Inclusion produces better products for everyone.
04
Build to understand
I prototype in React using Claude Code and Cursor. Understanding the material makes every design decision sharper.
